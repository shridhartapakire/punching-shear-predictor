CONCEPT

What is Punching Shear?

01

The Failure Mode

Punching shear is a structural failure where a concentrated load causes a column to punch through a flat slab, creating a cone-shaped fracture zone around the column perimeter — without prior visible warning.

02

Why It Occurs

When the punching shear stress (vu) acting over the critical perimeter exceeds the permissible shear stress (vc) of concrete, the slab fails. This is especially critical in flat slab buildings without drop panels or shear heads.

03

Structural Safety Importance

Punching shear failures are catastrophic and sudden. IS 456:2000 mandates checking vu ≤ vc = 0.25√fck for all flat slab column connections. This tool automates that check with precision.
